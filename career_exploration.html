Career Exploration

What is Career Exploration?

Career exploration is where you turn “I'm interested in a lot of things” into a focused direction and a realistic plan. Use this page to learn what different information careers look like, hear directly from alumni and recruiters, and identify which skills, experiences, and next steps will move you forward. Not sure where to start? Begin with the resources in the next section, then choose a career area below to dive deeper.

Explore by Career Area

Pick a path to explore. Each area includes panel recordings and job/internship search materials so you can understand both the work and how to get hired.

Data

Explore roles like data analyst, data scientist, analytics consultant, and data product roles. Start with alumni and recruiter sessions to understand common tools, portfolios, and what employers look for, then use the workshop slides to plan your next skill steps.

  • MSI Data Alumni Panel Winter 2024
  • Recruiter Panel: Data Analytics & Data Science Hiring Demystified W25
  • Job/Internship Search: Data

Library & Archives

Explore paths in libraries, archives, digital preservation, metadata, and cultural heritage organizations. Use the alumni panels to see the variety of workplaces and roles, then review the workshop slides to learn how internships and hiring differ from other tracks.

  • UMSI LAKES Alumni Panel W25 Links to an external site.
  • Job/Internship Search: Libraries & Archives

UX

Explore UX research, UX design, product design, and interaction design. Alumni panels help you understand team structures and day-to-day workflows; the workshop slides help you map out portfolios, case studies, and recruiting timelines.

  • MSI UX Alumni Panel Winter 2024 Links to an external site.
  • Job/Internship Search Series: UX Careers

Turn Exploration into Action

Once you’ve explored one or two paths, take action to build momentum.

  1. Choose your focus: Reviewing your interests and skills in alignment with the career paths
  2. Use career support: Schedule time with the Career Development Office to review your direction, refine your target roles, and plan your search strategy.
  3. Network with purpose: Attending workshops or events to build your network
